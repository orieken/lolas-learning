# 🌟 Weekly Slow-Down Practice Plan (1st Grade)

## Daily Ritual (2 minutes before schoolwork)
### 3 Balloon Breaths
    – Both of you take three deep breaths, filling your “balloon belly” and slowly letting the air out.
    - Cue Phrase – Say together: “I go slow, I check, I grow.” (short, fun mantra to anchor the behavior)

## Monday – Number Detective

### Activity: Write a short sequence of numbers (1–20 or 1–50) with mistakes (skipped, doubled).

* Her job: Circle the “errors” and explain what’s wrong.
* Purpose: Builds carefulness and attention to sequence.

## Tuesday – Freeze Math
### Activity: Give her 5–10 simple math problems.
* Call out “freeze!” a few times while she’s working. She must stop, point to where she is, take one breath, then continue.
* Purpose: Teaches pausing and self-awareness while working.

## Wednesday – Slow Motion Counting Game

### Activity: Count together to 30, but only aloud in slow motion (e.g., clap, stomp, or snap between each number).
Variation: Roll a die and count that many steps forward on a board or with toys.
Purpose: Reinforces not skipping numbers.

## Thursday – Check Box Challenge
### Activity: Add little empty checkboxes next to each problem on her worksheet (or write your own).

She must solve → check the box → move on.

Purpose: Creates a rhythm of solving and double-checking.

## Friday – Board Game Day
### Activity: Play a board game with dice and spaces (e.g., Chutes & Ladders, Sorry, Candy Land).
Rule: She has to move her piece slowly, one step at a time.
Purpose: Makes careful counting fun and social.

## Saturday – Mindful Coloring or Dot-to-Dot
### Activity: Do a dot-to-dot picture or a coloring page where she must color only one shape at a time before moving on.
Purpose: Patience, sequencing, and slowing down visually.

## Sunday – Detective Reading
### Activity: Read a short passage together. Every few lines, she stops and checks: “Did I skip a word? Did I say it twice?”
Add a little detective hat or magnifying glass for fun.
Purpose: Transfers “slow down” skill to reading, not just math.

## 🌈 Parent Tips
* Keep sessions short and playful – 5–15 minutes max.
* Praise effort, not just outcome: “I love how carefully you checked that line!”
* Use fun language: “Detective eyes,” “Freeze power,” “Slow-motion counting.”
* Over time, she’ll internalize the cues and use them during schoolwork.
